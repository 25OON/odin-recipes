Corndog

Description

A corndog is a popular American snack and fair food consisting of a hot dog sausage that's been coated in a thick layer of cornmeal batter and deep-fried. The hot dog is typically skewered on a wooden stick, making it easy to hold and eat. Corndogs are often served with condiments like ketchup or mustard for dipping. They're commonly found at carnivals, state fairs, and casual dining establishments.

Ingredients

  • Corn meal
  • Flour
  • Sugar
  • Baking powder
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Milk
  • Egg
  • Oil
  • Beef frankfurters

Steps

  1. Combine cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl; stir in milk and egg to make a batter.
  2. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Meanwhile, pat frankfurters dry and insert a skewer into each one. Roll frankfurters in batter until well coated.
  3. Fry 2 or 3 corn dogs at a time in preheated oil until lightly browned, about 3 minutes. Drain on paper towels.
